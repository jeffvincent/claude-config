# Advisory Board — Video Transcript Analyzer Personas

After completing the initial analysis, run the draft through these three reviewer personas. Synthesize their feedback into the final output.

---

## Persona 1: The Product Researcher

**Role:** Evaluates whether the analysis captures actionable product insights
**Priority:** Nothing important gets lost

**Evaluation lens:**
- Did the analysis capture every distinct pain point mentioned?
- Are feature requests and workarounds documented?
- Would a PM reading this know exactly what to build or fix?
- Are business impact statements (revenue, time, team size) preserved?
- Did the topical breakdown miss any significant theme?

**Key question:** "Could a product team prioritize work based solely on this analysis?"

**Red flags this persona catches:**
- Pain points mentioned in transcript but missing from topical breakdown
- Quotes that are paraphrased instead of verbatim
- Themes that are too broad ("UX issues" instead of "Search refresh during typing")
- Missing business context (customer said "$50K impact" but it's not in the analysis)

---

## Persona 2: The Quote Verifier

**Role:** Ensures absolute fidelity of all quoted material
**Priority:** Zero tolerance for paraphrasing

**Evaluation lens:**
- Cross-reference every quote in "Key Quotes" against the original transcript
- Check that no words were added, removed, or changed
- Verify speaker attribution is correct
- Confirm timestamps (if SRT) point to the right moment
- Check that the "best" quotes were selected (most impactful, not just first found)

**Key question:** "If the customer saw this analysis, would they say 'yes, that's exactly what I said'?"

**Red flags this persona catches:**
- Cleaned-up grammar that changes the speaker's voice
- Merged quotes from different parts of the conversation
- Missing context that makes a quote misleading
- Timestamps that are off by more than 10 seconds

---

## Persona 3: The Structure Editor

**Role:** Evaluates document organization and readability
**Priority:** Someone scanning this document gets value in 2 minutes

**Evaluation lens:**
- Does the summary stand alone? (Can you skip everything else and still understand?)
- Are topics ordered by importance, not just chronology?
- Is there unnecessary repetition between summary, quotes, and topical breakdown?
- Are bullet points specific enough? (No "discussed various issues")
- Does the document flow logically from summary → quotes → breakdown → transcript?

**Key question:** "Can a reader at any level of familiarity get exactly what they need?"

**Red flags this persona catches:**
- Summary that just lists topics without synthesizing
- Topical breakdown that reads like a transcript summary (too chronological)
- Bullets that are vague or could apply to any customer call
- Missing transition between the analysis sections and the raw transcript

---

## How to Use This Board

1. Complete the initial analysis using the standard workflow
2. Before finalizing, adopt each persona sequentially
3. For each persona, check the draft against their evaluation lens
4. Fix any issues found — especially Quote Verifier findings (zero tolerance)
5. If the Product Researcher flags a missing theme, add it to the topical breakdown
6. If the Structure Editor flags flow issues, reorganize
