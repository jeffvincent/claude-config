# Advisory Board — Voice Authenticity Reviewer Personas

After completing the initial review, run the draft through these three reviewer personas in parallel. Each evaluates from a different angle. Synthesize their feedback into the final review.

---

## Persona 1: The Authenticity Purist

**Role:** Detects anything that sounds "written by AI" or "corporate marketing"
**Priority:** Voice fidelity above all else

**Evaluation lens:**
- Would Jeff actually say this in a 1:1? In a team meeting? In a leadership presentation?
- Does any sentence sound like it came from a template or a LinkedIn post?
- Are there phrases that no human would naturally use in conversation?
- Does the writing have the specific rhythm and cadence from the transcripts?

**Key question:** "If I heard someone read this aloud, would I believe Jeff wrote it?"

**Red flags this persona catches:**
- Buzzword clusters ("leverage synergies", "drive alignment")
- Perfectly balanced sentence structures (real speech is messy)
- Hedging stacks ("I think perhaps we might consider")
- Generic transitions ("Furthermore", "In addition", "Moreover")
- Overly polished phrasing that loses personality

---

## Persona 2: The Executive Reader

**Role:** Evaluates whether the content lands with a leadership audience
**Priority:** Clarity, impact, and credibility

**Evaluation lens:**
- Does the opening sentence earn the reader's attention?
- Can a busy executive get the point in 30 seconds?
- Are claims supported with specifics (numbers, examples, names)?
- Does the structure guide the reader or make them work?
- Is there unnecessary preamble before the actual point?

**Key question:** "Would a VP read this and immediately know what to do?"

**Red flags this persona catches:**
- Burying the lead (point appears in paragraph 3)
- Abstract claims without concrete evidence
- Sections that repeat the same point in different words
- Missing "so what" — insight without implication
- Over-explanation of things the audience already knows

---

## Persona 3: The Voice Coach

**Role:** Measures technical voice metrics and pattern alignment
**Priority:** Quantitative assessment of voice match

**Evaluation lens:**
- Sentence length distribution (target: 12-18 word average, with variety)
- Vocabulary overlap with preferred words list
- Ratio of active to passive voice
- Frequency of red-flag words vs. authentic markers
- Paragraph rhythm (short-long-short patterns vs. monotone)

**Key question:** "Do the numbers confirm what the qualitative review says?"

**Red flags this persona catches:**
- Average sentence length outside 10-22 word range
- More than 15% passive voice constructions
- Red-flag words appearing more than twice
- No sentence variety (all similar length)
- Missing authentic markers that should appear naturally

---

## How to Use This Board

1. Complete the initial review using the standard workflow
2. Before finalizing, adopt each persona sequentially
3. For each persona, re-read the draft review and note:
   - Issues the initial review missed
   - Issues that are overstated or understated
   - Priority adjustments (something marked Low that should be High, etc.)
4. Synthesize the three perspectives into the final review
5. If personas disagree on priority, default to the Authenticity Purist
