# Good Example: Strategic Memo Voice Review

This is an annotated example of a well-executed voice authenticity review on a strategic memo.

## What makes this review good

1. **Every issue cites a specific pattern** — not just "this sounds off"
2. **Rewrites preserve meaning** while shifting to authentic voice
3. **Authentic moments are celebrated** — not just problems listed
4. **Quick wins are genuinely high-impact** — not random picks
5. **Metrics back up the qualitative assessment**

---

## Sample Review Excerpt

### 🔴 High Priority (Voice Breaker)

**1. Para 2, Sentence 1**
- **Current:** "It is imperative that we leverage our existing infrastructure to drive meaningful alignment across cross-functional stakeholders."
- **Issue:** Triple red flag — "imperative", "leverage", "drive alignment" are all on the avoid list. Passive construction ("It is") hides the actor. This sentence would never come out of Jeff's mouth.
- **Suggestion:** "We need to use what we've already built to get product, engineering, and CS on the same page."
- **Impact:** This is the opening of the section. If leadership reads one sentence of corporate-speak, they'll assume the rest is the same.

### ✅ Authentic Moment

**"The honest answer is we don't know yet — but here's what we're doing to find out."** (Para 5)
- **Why this works:** Matches the "direct admission of uncertainty + action" pattern from transcripts. Uses "honest answer" (preferred phrase). Short, punchy, and confident even while admitting a gap.
- **Strength:** This is exactly the kind of sentence that builds trust with leadership.

### 📊 Voice Metrics

| Metric | Current | Target | Assessment |
|--------|---------|--------|------------|
| Avg sentence length | 22 words | 12-18 words | Needs work — too long |
| Vocabulary match | 68% | 80%+ | Needs work |
| Tone consistency | Mixed | Consistent | Drift in paras 2-4 |
| Pattern alignment | 4 matches | Strong | Moderate |

**Assessment:** The memo starts strong (para 1 is authentic), drifts into corporate mode in the middle (paras 2-4), then recovers in the closing. The middle section needs the most work.
