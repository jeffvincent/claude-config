# Admin Setup Journey Research Analysis

## Metadata
- **Study Name**: Admin Setup Journey
- **Created By**: Julie Strubel with Carolyn Griffel, UX Research, CRM
- **Study URL**: https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1HK08I4wV7MdO3sFhOH-m5p_8ZCTmbfzfC13xF6bDJNs/edit#slide=id.g12419aaebf7_1_2785

## The Big Picture

This research explores how upmarket companies (200+ employees) approach the setup process for HubSpot's CRM and reveals that it's a complex, long-term endeavor fraught with challenges. The findings align with HubSpot's broader data strategy of creating a unified customer platform, but highlight significant gaps in the current implementation process. While HubSpot aims to make sophisticated operations "easy, fast, and unified," the reality for admins is quite different - they face steep learning curves, time pressures, and limited guidance during a process that typically spans 4-6 months to 2-3 years.

The research demonstrates that poor initial setup decisions can lead to months of rework and diminished data quality, directly impacting HubSpot's value proposition as a unified data platform. This connects to the company's strategic transformation toward becoming a complete data platform ("Data Hub") that enables effective data management and activation, but shows that significant improvements are needed in guiding users through the complex initial setup phase.

## Key Findings

### 1. Long-Term Setup Process
- Upmarket companies view CRM implementation as a phased effort spanning 4-6 months to 2-3 years
- Setup complexity compounds over time as additional hubs and departments are integrated
- Knowledge transfer gaps occur as personnel and consultants change throughout the extended implementation
- Significant consensus building is required during both sales and post-activation phases

### 2. "Learning While Doing" Challenge
- Admins face steep learning curves with immediate pressure to show ROI
- Admin skill and knowledge levels vary greatly, but all are expected to become HubSpot experts immediately
- HubSpot's hand-holding (through onboarding experts) helps but doesn't extend through the full setup process
- Admins often make crucial setup decisions without awareness of downstream consequences

### 3. "Getting It Wrong" Risk
- Setup errors lead to frustration and months of rework
- Many admins don't fully understand the data model concept, complicated by HubSpot's unique terminology
- Admins don't fully utilize functionality they're paying for due to overwhelming workload and lack of awareness
- Incremental corrections often add to issues rather than resolving fundamental setup problems

### 4. End-User Onboarding Criticality
- Smooth onboarding experiences are essential for persuading reluctant stakeholders and end-users
- Admins often "remake" onboarding experiences to suit different audiences without expertise in change management
- Failed onboarding lowers adoption, increases data errors, and reduces reporting accuracy
- Creating effective onboarding materials is time-consuming and often beyond admin capabilities

## Recommendations

### Strategic Initiatives
1. **Create Interactive Setup Roadmaps**
   - Develop multi-hub CRM roadmap tools to ensure smart phasing and growth
   - Make roadmaps visible to all stakeholders and consultants
   - Include visualization of setup steps, functionality, integrations, and timelines

2. **Establish In-App Guidance Framework**
   - Implement in-app contextual guidance for common CRM setup errors
   - Add verification mechanisms to confirm when setup tasks are completed correctly
   - Provide best practices and templates that guide setup actions for admins of all skill levels

3. **Develop End-User Enablement Tools**
   - Create customizable onboarding modules admins can adapt for their end-users
   - Implement in-app guides that coach end-users through standard tasks and processes
   - Provide end-user training templates focused on role-specific use cases

### Tactical Actions
1. **Implement Decision Support Systems**
   - Add warning systems for potential setup issues (e.g., "Are you sure this isn't the same pipeline?")
   - Create guided setups for complex features like pipelines, data models, and reporting
   - Offer ROI tracking tools to help admins demonstrate value quickly

2. **Improve Knowledge Transfer**
   - Build approval workflows for stakeholder setup sign-off to ensure alignment
   - Provide in-app instruction on key HubSpot concepts to accelerate learning curve
   - Create better documentation of setup decisions for future admins

3. **Streamline Setup Experience**
   - Put CRM setup tasks in the context of use cases (start with business goal reporting and work backward)
   - Focus on end-to-end business processes rather than isolated features
   - Connect initial setup decisions to reporting needs to prevent data gaps

## Connected Research

### 1. "Analysis: What Unified Data Means to Marketing Leaders"
This research connects directly to findings about data unification challenges. While marketing leaders need strategic data separation and selective data surfacing, the admin setup journey research shows that achieving this balance is difficult without proper guidance and understanding of the data model. Both studies point to the need for better data modeling tools and clearer implementation paths.

### 2. "Data Model Support Research Analysis"
The admin setup findings strongly align with data model research, particularly regarding the data literacy gap and difficulty in translating business needs into technical setup. Both studies highlight how initial mistakes in data structure create long-term problems, and both emphasize the need for guided setup experiences and industry templates.

### 3. "Ops/Admin User Impact Analysis"
This research provides context for the importance of successful admin setup. With only 14% of users classified as ops/admin but their presence strongly correlated with healthy GRR (Gross Revenue Retention), ensuring their success is critical. The admin setup journey research identifies the specific pain points these crucial users face during implementation.

### 4. "Analysis: A Framework for Aligning Admin Experience Efforts"
This framework research complements the admin setup findings by proposing a task-centered approach to admin experiences. The admin setup journey provides concrete examples of how the proposed framework could address real implementation challenges, particularly in the areas of data administration, portal oversight, and software administration.

## Notes on This Example

**What makes this a strong analysis:**

1. **HTML Comment Summary**: Provides instant context - type, themes, insights, relevance, quality
2. **The Big Picture**: Connects research to strategy (Data Hub, SO5), explains why it matters
3. **Key Findings**: Grouped by themes, specific details, clear implications
4. **Recommendations Split**: Strategic (long-term, cross-functional) vs Tactical (near-term, actionable)
5. **Connected Research**: Specific studies from catalog, explicit connections explained
6. **Slide Index**: Grouped by theme for searchability
7. **Specificity**: Timeline ranges (4-6 months to 2-3 years), specific challenges, concrete examples

**How to adapt this for different research types:**

- **Quantitative Studies**: Add "Interesting Metrics" section with key statistics
- **JTBD Research**: Focus on job themes and opportunity scores in Key Findings
- **Market Analysis**: Emphasize competitive insights and market trends
- **Product Testing**: Include usability findings and design recommendations

**Common adjustments:**

- Longer reports may need more Key Findings subsections
- Some research may require "Disconnected Research" if contradictions exist
- Slide Index format varies (slides vs sections vs chapters)
- Recommendations may be more strategic-heavy or tactical-heavy depending on research scope
